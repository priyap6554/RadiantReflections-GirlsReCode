Mental Health

Now more than ever, diversity and inclusion for women and gender-nonconforming people in mental health/wellness is critical for addressing the needs of our community. Join us in our mission to help fight the stigma and empower women and gender minorities to nurture their mental wellbeing!




  • Mental Health Issues Disproportionately Affect Women + Gender Minorities

    Several factors, including societal gender roles, healthcare discrimination, stigma surrounding mental health, and healthcare inaccess contribute to and exacerbate mental health issues for women and gender minorities. These communities have unique challenges that are often not adequately addressed in healthcare and wellness spaces, especially for people of color. Scroll below to read a brief overview and learn more.



  • Women's Mental Health

    Women are 3 times more likely to develop mental illnesses in comparison to men. They are also 2 times more likely to develop anxiety, and self harm rates have tripled since the 1990s. Healthcare disparities and discrimination often discourage or hinder women from recieving proper treatment. Related statistics, such as 81% of all women being sexually harrassed, shed light on the society's views and effects on women, which contribute to the growing mental health issues.




  • Mental Health For Gender Minorities

    Transgender people are 4 times more likely than those who are cisgender to have mental health problems. Trans healthcare is widely restricted accross many states and internationally as well, and transphobia in schools, family, and other community circles makes it difficult for transgender people to be accepted and lead healthy lives. Trans people regularly experience harrassment, violence, and mistreatment, leading to a need for greater mental health care and equity for the community.
